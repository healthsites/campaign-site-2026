<p style="color:#585858;line-height:1.8;margin:0 0 22px;font-size:17px">The Saint-Louis campaign was launched during the COVID-19 pandemic through the WHO–UNICEF GIS Working Group for COVAX. A team of 12 local OpenStreetMap community members validated health facilities across the medical district over three months, producing one of the most complete open health facility datasets in West Africa.</p>

<h3>The user story that drove the work</h3>

<blockquote class="campaign-quote">As a pregnant mother I want to know where the nearest emergency health service is, so that I can plan for potential complications during childbirth.</blockquote>

<p style="color:#585858;line-height:1.8;margin:0 0 36px">From 397 facilities visited, 35 were identified as likely to support emergency pregnancy referrals — based on facility type, bed availability, emergency health status, and contact information.</p>

<h3>Étape 2 — Evidence and Policy Engagement</h3>

<p style="color:#585858;line-height:1.8;margin:0 0 22px">Étape 1 produced the data. Étape 2 puts it to work — through clinical confirmation of the 35 emergency facilities, a formal NSO–MoH–OSM interoperability framework, and an evidence-based case to the Ministry of Finance for open data as permanent national infrastructure.</p>
